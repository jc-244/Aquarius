Sinusoids in Terms of Exponentials: Euler’s Formula

Sinusoids can be expressed in terms of exponentials using Euler’s formula [see Eq. (B.3)]

$$\cos \varphi=\frac{1}{2}\left(e^{j\varphi}+e^{-j\varphi}\right) \qquad \text{(B.24a)}$$

$$\sin \varphi=\frac{1}{2j}\left(e^{j\varphi}-e^{-j\varphi}\right) \qquad \text{(B.24b)}$$

Inversion of these equations yields

$$e^{j\varphi}=\cos \varphi+j\sin \varphi \qquad \text{(B.25a)}$$
$$e^{-j\varphi}=\cos \varphi-j\sin \varphi \qquad \text{(B.25b)}$$
