COMPLEX ROOTS
The procedure for handling complex roots is the same as that for real roots. For complex roots, the usual procedure leads to complex characteristic modes and the complex form of solution. However, it is possible to avoid the complex form altogether by selecting a real-form of solution, as described next.

For a real system, complex roots must occur in pairs of conjugates if the coefficients of the characteristic polynomial Q(О») are to be real. Therefore, if Оұ + jОІ is a characteristic root, Оұ вҲ’ jОІ must also be a characteristic root. The zero-input response corresponding to this pair of complex conjugate roots is

y_0(t) = c_1e^{(Оұ+jОІ)t} + c_2e^{(ОұвҲ’jОІ)t}                                                    (2.7)

For a real system, the response y_0(t) must also be real. This is possible only if c_1 and c_2 are conjugates. Let

c_1 = (c/2)e^{jОё}    and    c_2 = (c/2)e^{вҲ’jОё}

This yields

y_0(t) = (c/2)e^{jОё}e^{(Оұ+jОІ)t} + (c/2)e^{вҲ’jОё}e^{(ОұвҲ’jОІ)t}
